Diabetes

The term diabetes is associated with frequent urination and thirst. It is known to affect people of all ages. There are different forms of it, the most common being diabetes type 2 or diabetes mellitus. Let us understand it in simple words.

01
The fuel

Imagine that your body is a car which requires fuel to work and also keys to start. Just like cars require petrol/diesel to move forward, our bodies use glucose as a source of energy. We obtain glucose through the food we eat or drink.

02
The key

Insulin is a hormone produced by pancreas and acts like a key which unlocks our cells so the fuel of our body or glucose can reach inside the cells and in turn provide energy.

03
The key problem

Just like car glitches there can be glitches in our body systems. The diabetes glitch can happen when enough insulin is not produced by pancreas or when our body cells do not respond to it properly.

04
The Traffic of High Blood Sugar

The efficiency of our cells to use glucose decreases if there is less amount of insulin or if it does not work properly. Hence, glucose can accumulate in our bloodstream and cause a traffic jam of high blood sugar.

05
Back in Control: Handling Diabetes

Individuals who have diabetes can regulate their blood sugar levels with proper medication and healthcare support. With proper care and maintenance, you can keep your body-car running smoothly while enjoying the ride of your life!

Diabetes Symptoms

  • Frequent urination
  • Frequent thirst
  • Increased hunger
  • Numbness in feet or hands
  • Blurred vision
  • Dry skin
  • Sores heal slower than usual
  • Increased tiredness

Myth 1

Getting Diabetes is as simple as catching a cold!

Fact: Diabetes is not contagious. It is a non-communicable disease that cannot be spread by casual contact, by sharing food or by exposure to a diabetic person. Genetic factors and personal health decisions, including food and exercise, play a combined role in the development of diabetes.

Myth 2

Diabetes is a curable condition!

Fact: Diabetes can be adequately treated, but it cannot be cured. A healthy lifestyle that includes a balanced diet, frequent exercise, and appropriate medical treatment can help people with diabetes manage their blood sugar levels, even though there is no known cure.

Myth 3

No family history means no diabetes!

Fact:Despite the widespread belief that personal immunity is guaranteed by a family free of diabetes cases, the complexity of diabetes highlights the significance of taking into account factors other than the health records of one's immediate relatives.

Myth 4

Diabetes is an inevitable ticket to sightless eyes and limb loss!

Fact: Contrary to popular assumption, diabetes does not always steal your vision or mobility. Adequate management of diabetes considerably lowers the likelihood of these issues. Frequent screenings of eyes and feet are necessary for keeping an eye on things and identifying possible problems early on.
